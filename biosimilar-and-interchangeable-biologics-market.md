<p><p>Biosimilars are biologic medical products that are highly similar to an already approved reference biologic in terms of safety, efficacy, and quality, despite minor differences. Interchangeable biologics are biosimilars that meet additional regulatory standards, demonstrating that they can be substituted for the reference product without prescriber intervention. The market for Biosimilars and Interchangeable Biologics is witnessing significant growth driven by several factors, including increasing chronic disease prevalence, the expiration of patents for key biologics, and rising healthcare costs.</p><p>The demand for cost-effective therapies has prompted healthcare providers and payers to consider biosimilars as viable alternatives to expensive reference biologics. Additionally, advancements in biotechnology and regulatory support have facilitated the development and approval of biosimilars, enhancing market access. Key trends include increasing payer coverage for biosimilars, expanding biosimilar portfolios from manufacturers, and greater awareness among healthcare practitioners and patients regarding the benefits of these products. As competition increases, the market landscape is evolving with a focus on innovation and patient-centric approaches.</p></p>
