<p><ul><li>Active Torque Vectoring System (ATVS)</li><li>Passive Torque Vectoring System (PTVS)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive torque vectoring systems market comprises two main types: Active Torque Vectoring Systems (ATVS) and Passive Torque Vectoring Systems (PTVS). ATVS use electronic controls to dynamically distribute power between wheels for enhanced traction and stability, particularly in challenging driving conditions. In contrast, PTVS relies on mechanical components and the vehicle's inherent dynamics to achieve similar outcomes. Both systems improve handling and performance, but ATVS offers more precision and adaptability, making it increasingly popular in modern vehicles.</p></p>
