<p><p>Gadolinium-Based Contrast Agents (GBCAs) are used in medical imaging, primarily in MRI procedures, to enhance the visibility of internal structures. The market is segmented into two main types: Linear GBCAs and Macrocyclic GBCAs. Linear GBCAs are often simpler in structure but have a higher risk of releasing gadolinium into the body, leading to potential toxicity. In contrast, Macrocyclic GBCAs have a more stable structure, reducing the risk of gadolinium leakage, making them generally safer for patients.</p></p>
