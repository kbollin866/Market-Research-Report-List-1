<p><p>The horizontal forging machine market is primarily categorized into hot forging and cold forging segments. Hot forging involves shaping metal at elevated temperatures, enhancing malleability and allowing for complex shapes, often used in industries like automotive and aerospace. Cold forging, on the other hand, occurs at room temperature, resulting in denser and stronger parts due to strain hardening, commonly utilized in fasteners and smaller components. Both methods cater to diverse industrial needs, reflecting varying production requirements and material properties.</p></p>
